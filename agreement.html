8. MANAGEMENT FEE STRUCTURE
8.1 Management Fee Schedule:
AUM Tier Management Fee
First $100,000 2.5% annually
$100,001 - $500,000 2.0% annually
Above $500,000 1.5% annually
8.2 Fee Calculation:

Management Fees shall be calculated quarterly in arrears and deducted from the portfolio.

8.3 Management Fee Coverage:
  • Portfolio management and rebalancing
  • Market research and analysis
  • Operational expenses
  • Compliance and reporting
  • Administrative costs
9. PERFORMANCE FEE
9.1 Fee Structure:

The Company shall be entitled to a Performance Fee of 15% on profits exceeding a 6% annual hurdle rate.

9.2 Performance Fee Calculation:
  • Only charged on profits above the 6% benchmark
  • Calculated annually
  • High-water mark applies
  • No performance fee in loss-making years
9.3 Example Calculation:

If portfolio returns 18% in a year:

• First 6%: No performance fee

• Next 12%: 15% performance fee on this portion

• Performance Fee = 12% × 15% = 1.8% of AUM

10. FUND WATERFALL & PROFIT DISTRIBUTION
10.1 Distribution Order:
  1. Return of capital to Investor
  2. 6% preferred return to Investor
  3. Catch-up to Company to equal 15% of profits above hurdle
  4. 85%/15% split between Investor and Company on remaining profits
10.2 Actual Distribution:

• Investor receives: 85% of profits above 6% hurdle

• Company receives: 15% of profits above 6% hurdle + Management Fee

11. LOSS ALLOCATION & RECOVERY
11.1 Loss Allocation:

Losses are borne 100% by the Investor until capital is reduced.

11.2 Loss Recovery:
  • Future profits first used to recover Investor's capital losses
  • Then to achieve 6% preferred return
  • Only then do performance fees resume
12. NO GUARANTEED RETURNS & ALIGNMENT OF INTERESTS

The Investor acknowledges that all returns are market-based and dependent on investment performance. Historical returns are not indicative of future performance.

The fee structure ensures alignment:

  • Company shares in profits only after Investor achieves 6% return
  • Management fees ensure operational sustainability
  • Performance fees incentivize superior returns

APPENDIX B: FEE CALCULATION EXAMPLES
Example 1: $100,000 Investment with 18% Return

• Management Fee: $2,500 (2.5% of AUM)

• Hurdle Rate (6%): $6,000

• Excess Return: $18,000 - $6,000 = $12,000

• Performance Fee: $12,000 × 15% = $1,800

• Investor Net: $18,000 - $2,500 - $1,800 = $13,700

• Net Return to Investor: 13.7%

Example 2: $300,000 Investment with 12% Return

• Management Fee: $6,000 (2.0% of AUM)

• Hurdle Rate (6%): $18,000

• Excess Return: $36,000 - $18,000 = $18,000

• Performance Fee: $18,000 × 15% = $2,700

• Investor Net: $36,000 - $6,000 - $2,700 = $27,300

• Net Return to Investor: 9.1%
